01 — Essence

The brand in one breath.

Petsona is a single calm place for everything pet — breed, vitals, vet records, behavioral notes, an AI-curated weekly care plan. The name is the promise: every pet has a Petsona, and the app captures it.

Every pet has a Petsona.

Voice

Premium-clean meets playful-quirky. Friendly and modern, not scholarly or fashion-y. Speaks to a first-time owner without dumbing down for a 10-year veteran. Think "your friend who's a vet tech" — never "premium wellness brand," never "pet store loyalty card."

Animation principle

Animated everywhere. Every screen transition, every list reveal, every tap. Owning a pet is fun; the app should never feel boring. Motion tokens live in code (lib/theme.ts) — never hard-coded. Reduce-motion is respected; every animation drops to instant when the user has it on.

Three principles

One. Forest does the work. The dark forest green is the color your thumb presses. Primary CTAs, emphasis, the body of the wordmark.
Two. Honey does the recognition. The warm amber is the brand color — logo accents, badges, the moment your eye catches the brand.
Three. Ivory replaces white. Warm cream backgrounds reads "competent friend" where cold white reads "telehealth."

02 — Logo

The mark.

A continuous-line composition: a dog sitting on the left, a cat sitting on the right, their backs touching. Hand-drawn outline with a brush-painted character. Both species visible at first glance — no cat owner ever feels secondary to the mark.

Vectorization

Source: logo_mockup.png (the locked design). Vectorized via OpenCV contour detection (4 contours: dog outline, dog inner-ear curl, cat outline, ground line), smoothed with Catmull-Rom cubic Bezier interpolation. Smooth at any zoom level; preserves the hand-drawn brush-painted character.

Clear space

Half the height of the dog's head on every side. At minimum. Don't crowd it.

Minimum size

32px height for the full logo. Below that, use the app icon (the logo cropped + padded on the forest backdrop) — never shrink the full logo past 32px height.

Forest on ivory or honey backgrounds — the default brand mark.

Black for print fallback, fax, single-color photocopying.
White on dark print collateral or when laser-cutting / embossing.

Loading

Static TTF files in assets/fonts/DMSans-{Regular,Medium,SemiBold,Bold}.ttf → registered via useFonts in app/_layout.tsx. Splash held until fonts resolve to prevent flash of unstyled text.

Localization slack

Reserve 30% extra horizontal space on every text container. ES is typically 20–25% longer than EN; RU is similar with different break points. Numerals and product names (e.g., "Petsona") never translate.

Easing curves

Primary · cubic-bezier(0.32, 0.72, 0.0, 1) — almost all transitions. Calm, settled.
Out · cubic-bezier(0.5, 0.0, 0.4, 1) — dismiss / exit. Brisker.

Where animations live

Splash · logo scales 0.92 → 1.0 over 420ms; ivory bg crossfades to ivory app bg.
Screen enter · calmFade (260ms) on the container.
Card lists · fadeInUp(12) per child, 60ms stagger.
Sheets · sheetIn from bottom (420ms), sheetOut (260ms).
Tap feedback · useTapAnim() — scale 0.985 spring-back.
Streaming reveals · fadeInUp(20) per day card with stagger; honey shimmer on skeletons.

10 — Do / don't

The rules that protect the brand.

Do

  • Use the wordmark when there's room. Use the icon when there isn't.
  • Use forest for the thing your thumb presses. Use honey for the thing your eye recognizes.
  • Animate every state change — it's the brand promise.
  • Reserve 30% horizontal slack on every label for ES / RU.
  • Verify AA contrast against the table in §06.
  • Use Lucide icons. Line weight 1.8–2px.
  • Pick the monochrome variant that contrasts with the surface — never the same family.
  • Keep ivory backgrounds. Cold white is forbidden.

Don't

  • Don't put the wordmark on the app icon. Icon is logo-only.
  • Don't use honey-default for body text on ivory — fails AA at 2.8:1.
  • Don't tilt the wordmark. Don't curve it. Don't outline it.
  • Don't replace DM Sans with serifs, Comic Sans, or handwriting fonts.
  • Don't add a tagline beneath the wordmark in app chrome (marketing only).
  • Don't scatter paw prints. The dog + cat composition is the only pet glyph.
  • Don't use clinical-blue for any accent. Petsona is warm.
  • Don't gradient-fill the icon backdrop. Solid forest only.
